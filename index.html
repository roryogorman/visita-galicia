A Coruña

The most popular tourist destination of Galicia, the province of A Coruna boasts a fascinating mix of mile upon mile of picturesque beaches & quaint fishing villages, and breath taking inland scenery with meadows, mountains and forests. It is certainly the province that has the most to offer the holiday maker if you want a mix of coastal and inland attractions. The two largest cities of Galicia (a Coruna & the regions capital, Santiago de Compostela) both hold great attraction for the visitor – impressive architecture, a plethora of narrow streets housing the finest restaurants, many seasonal festivals & bars and with a population eager to welcome you. Leaving these cities behind, the magnificent ‘Costa del Morte’ (‘The Coast of Death’ – called after the numerous shipwrecks on this wild stretch of Atlantic coastline) will leave you breathless. All told if you want to sample what many tour operators now call "real" or "green" Spain, the province of A Coruna is probably the best place to start.

Our Top 3 Picks!

The Death Coast

Hundreds of kilometers of wild Atlantic coast stretching west from A Coruna. Here we find deserted beaches, coves and cliffs. Its capes, such as those of Touriñán, Vilán & Finisterre jut out into a wild choppy ocean. Spectacular Cape Finisterre - so called as the Romans believed it to be the edge of the world - is also the finish for many people's Camino de Santiago journey. Feel the natural energy of the spot and you will feel invigorated!

A Coruna

The bustling coastal city of A Coruna is the live heartbeat of the province. Its beautiful setting along a natural bay, its glass fronted buildings along the sea front, the impressive plazas and historic buildings in the centre will draw you in. During the day, the narrow streets housing leading stores, restaurants and bars. While at night, the city comes alive with its electric nightlife!

Cathedral of Santiago de Compostela

The stunning cathedral of the Galicia capital is the most famous building in the province. Attracting tourists from all overthe global, as well as thousands of Camino pilgrims looking to get their card stamped, this magnificent 15th century building is not to be missed, especially for Jacobeo in July each 7 years!

Pontevedra

Pontevedra occupies the south-western section of Galicia – bordered by the other 3 provinces to the north & east, the wild expanse of the Atlantic to the west & Portugal to the south. Due to its location, it enjoys its own microclimate – similar to Canarian weather – and this influences life in this area. The bustling port of Vigo is the main urban centre, but other settlements such as Pontvedra & Tui offers so much for tourism – historic monuments, stunning plazas and many of Galicia’s top eateries. The Atlantic coastline boasts some of Galicia’s finest beaches and offshore islands, indeed the beaches of San Xenxo & the Cies Islands are the envy of many parts of Spain. Seafood from this region is world famous, and Pontevedra also hosts the premier vineyards of the Galicia. Many festivals are held in the towns throughout the year, so no matter where you go, you will always find a hive of activity!

Our Top 3 Picks!

Adega Eidos Wine Tour

Pontevedra is home to the famous 'Albarino' wines - a lightly sparkling white wine, and one of the finest examples of such a winery is at Adega Eidos, near thr prtty coastal town of San Xenxo. Take in a tour of the 'bodega', see how the gapes are harvested and fermented to produce the wines, sample some of the delicious bouquets and take some home as a memeory of your visit!

Vigo

The busy, lively port city of Vigo is the largest settlement in Pontevedra province and is known locally as the city that doesn't sleep! Spend some hours along the busy port front, with its many tapas restaurants seving the freshest seafood. Spend some time exploring the charming old town, its numerous historuc monuments & buildings before engaging with the bust nightlife!

Las Islas Cies

The stunning Islas Cies, situated within the Vigo estuary are a little slice of tropical tranquility set in the terrifyingly-name Coast of Death! A natural park archipeligo, take a trip to witness flora and fauna, not usually associated with an oceanic coastline. Hike along the numerous trails on the islands before relaxing on the many remote beaches dotting the landscape.

Lugo

The district of Lugo is Galicia's largest and most northern province – bordering the beautiful community of Asturias to the east and with approx. 100km coastline to the north. The ancient walled city of Lugo – a UNESCO site – is a must-see, but the other settlements of Sarria & Monforte ae not to be missed. The ‘hilliest’ of the provinces, Lugo is a hikers dream, with many trails criss-crossing the region. Although having a relatively short coastline, the rock formations of As Cateidras attract large number of domestic & foreign tourists year-round.

Our Top 3 Picks!

Lugo City Walls

The Roman Walls of Lugo (Muralla Romana) are a defining feature of the city of Lugo. Designated a UNESCO World Heritage site in 2000, the Roman walls of Lugo are considered to be ‘the finest surviving example of late Roman military fortifications’. Take a walk along the walls and the many gates and breathe in some of the hostory of his beautiful city!

Mondoneda Cathedral

This stunning cathedral set in the town of Mondoneda will captivate you. A National Monument since 1902, it's peculiar with its perfect proportions and its low height, which is why it is nicknamed the ''kneeling cathedral''. This charming town is also a perfect place to relax - have a coffee in its central plaza with the cathedral as your vista!

As Catedrais

This breathtaking beach on Lugo's northern coast, facing the open expanse of the North Atlantic is home to a stunning series of rock formations known as 'The Cathedrals', so called for their majestic size and reverance. Hike along the many expansive trails that hug the coast, or take a walk out to the formations at low tide - we guarantee you will not be disappointed!

Ourense

Ourense is Galicia’s only inland province and until recently, the least-visited province. However this in turn means that a visit here is to see Galicia at its rawest! Its main settlement – Ourense – has a smaller population than Vigo, but in common with other towns of the province, is still relatively untouched by large scale tourism, Nature is at its finest here – from the stunning gorges of Los Balcones de Madrid, to the secret ski-slopes of Manzaneda (yes, ski-slopes!). Some of Galicia’s finest examples of historic monuments, churches and monuments are here, with some of the finest architecture in all of Spain. And, don’t forget to sample the famous ‘chupitos’ (liquers) of the region!

Our Top 3 Picks!

Las Balcones de Madrid

In the eastern hills of Ourense province sits a series of gorges carved out by the Sil river - the most specacular of these are the 'Balcones de Madrid' - high steep walls sweeping down to the river, it is one of the most popular destinations for visitors to the region. Hike amongst the many trails, or for the extra-adventurous descend down to the valley floor!

Manzaneda

Winter Sports? In this part of Spain? Yes! Manzaneda is one of the best-kept secrets of snow-loving Spaniards and its hosts some of the best facilities in the country outside of the Pyrenees. During the summer, the area boasts blissful, tranquil, but beautiful hiking trails, all less than hour outside of Ourense!

Pena Trevinca

Pena Trevinca - Galicia's highest point - sits on the border of Galicia and the neighbouring region of Leon. Not for the foolhardy, its a tough hike, but is ultimately rewarding for the scenery & beauty of the landscape around! The sense of achievement when you reach the summit will make the effort so worthwhile!
